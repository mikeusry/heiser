# Office Manager Oliver

> Responsible for keeping the office clean and employees happy—needs a partner, not just a vendor

**Annual Value:** $6,000–$24,000
**Primary Service:** Commercial/office cleaning

---

## Demographics

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Age | 30–55 |
| Company Size | 10–100 employees |
| Industries | Professional services, medical offices, small manufacturing |
| Location | Lake County IL business parks, downtown areas |
| Role | Office Manager, Facilities Manager, Operations Director, or small business owner |

---

## Who He Is

Oliver is responsible for keeping the workplace running smoothly. A dirty office reflects poorly on him, and he hears about it when employees complain. He's not a cleaning expert—he just wants a partner who handles it so he doesn't have to think about it.

He's dealt with unreliable vendors before and values **communication**. If something goes wrong, he wants to know about it and see it fixed quickly. He appreciates vendors who are proactive rather than reactive.

---

## Pain Points

1. Previous cleaners missed areas or did surface-level work
2. Dealing with employee complaints about cleanliness
3. Coordinating cleaning schedule around business hours
4. Finding cleaners who understand commercial needs
5. Getting blamed when the office isn't clean

---

## Decision Triggers

- Health inspector visit or audit coming up
- New employees or clients visiting the office
- Current vendor just dropped the ball badly
- Lease renewal requiring clean facility
- COVID/illness outbreak at the office

---

## What He Values

- Accountability and communication
- Flexibility with scheduling
- Consistent quality every visit
- Problem-solving attitude

---

## What He Fears

- Getting locked into a long contract
- Quality declining after the first month
- Security concerns with cleaning staff
- Being nickel-and-dimed for extras

---

## His Decision Process

1. Gets fed up with current vendor or inherits the task
2. Asks for recommendations from other office managers
3. Gets 2–3 quotes
4. Looks for responsiveness and professionalism in the quoting process
5. Starts with a trial period before committing
